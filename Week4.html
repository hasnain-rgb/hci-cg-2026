Interaction Analysis: Norman and GOMS Applied to the “Who Paid?” App

Applying Norman’s 7 Stages of Action

Task: User wants to check who paid last.

  1. Form Goal – “I want to know who paid for the last expense.”
  2. Form Intention – “I will check the app to find the last payer.”
  3. Specify Action – “I will open the app and tap the history/recent payments section.”
  4. Execute Action – The user opens the app and taps the relevant button.
  5. Perceive State – The app displays a list of transactions.
  6. Interpret State – The user reads the name and date to identify who paid last.
  7. Evaluate Outcome – The user confirms whether the goal is satisfied.

Reference: Dix et al., Human–Computer Interaction, Chapter 9

Applying GOMS

Goal

Find out who paid last.

Operators (Basic Actions)

  • Tap app icon
  • Tap “History/Recent Payments”
  • Scroll list
  • Read names and timestamps

Method (One Possible Way)

Open app → Go to recent payments → Scroll → Read latest entry

Selection Rules

  • If “Recent Payments” is visible on the home screen, tap it.
  • Else, open the menu and select “History”.

Reference: Dix et al., Human–Computer Interaction, Chapter 12

How Norman and GOMS Differ in This Case

Differences Between Norman and GOMS for the “Who Paid?” App
Model What it Explains in This App
Norman Why users may struggle due to gulfs of execution and evaluation and unclear feedback.
GOMS What steps users take and how the task is structured.
KLM (Optional) How long it takes to perform each step.

Typical Problems Found

  • Norman (Gulf of Execution): Users don’t know where to tap to see who paid.
  • Norman (Gulf of Evaluation): The list does not clearly show “Last paid by”.
  • GOMS: Too many steps (menu → history → scroll) increase task time.

Simple Design Improvements

  • Add a clear label: “Last Paid By: ___” on the home screen.
  • Make “Recent Payments” a primary button.
  • Highlight the latest transaction.
